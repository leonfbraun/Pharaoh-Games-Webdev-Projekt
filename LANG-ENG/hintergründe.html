Profile Tutanchamun

Before his discovery, he was one of the least known kings of ancient Egypt. But under his rule, Egypt officially found, after the heresy of Akhenaten, back to his old gods. Tutankhamun grew up in quite a turbulent Time on, his predecessor Akhenaten had broken centuries of traditional rules and thus threw Egypt's rule out of balance for a good two to three decades. Even if you don't know the details about your origins, it still applies Tutankhamun as the son of Pharaoh Akhenaten (Amenhotep IV) and his concubine Kiya.

Tutankhamun's tomb is the only royal tomb in the Valley of the Kings that has not been plundered represent; and the golden sarcophagi, the unique jewels and the rest Funerary equipment, today form one of the great tourist attractions of Egypt Museum in Cairo. Tutankhamun's tomb was once in the Pharaonic period broken open, but without suffering major damage; some items were missing otherwise the tomb was intact.
